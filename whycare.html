Health Risk

Exposure to extreme heat can cause exhaustion, heat stroke, kidney and heart disease. Since climate change harms about plants and our food security, this will lead to sharply rising food prices or no food, hunger, starvation, and emigration.

Temperature

Earth's temperature has risen by an average of 0.14° Fahrenheit (0.08° Celsius) per decade since 1880. This is because these greenhouse gas emissions have increased the greenhouse effect and caused the earth's surface temperature to rise.

Air Pollution

Climate change can affect air quality, and certain air pollutants can affect climate change. For example, hot sunny days associated with a warming climate can increase ground-level ozone in some areas
