Why Care

Health Risk

Exposure to extreme heat can cause exhaustion, heat stroke, kidney and heart disease

Temperature

Earth's temperature has risen by an average of 0.14° Fahrenheit (0.08° Celsius) per decade since 1880

Air Pollution

Climate change can affect air quality, and certain air pollutants can affect climate change. For example, hot sunny days associated with a warming climate can increase ground-level ozone in some areas
